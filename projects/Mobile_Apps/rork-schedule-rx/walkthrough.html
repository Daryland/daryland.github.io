📖 How-To Guide

Know Before You Dose

Peptide handling is a skill. The How-To Guide walks you through the complete reconstitution process, correct storage, and essential best practices — everything you need to handle these compounds safely and effectively.

⚠

Legal Disclaimer: This information is provided for educational and research purposes only and does not constitute medical advice. Research peptides are not approved by the FDA for human use. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional and comply with all applicable laws and regulations.

Reconstitution Process

1
Prepare Your Materials Gather peptide vial, bacteriostatic water (BAC water), alcohol swabs, and syringes. Wash hands thoroughly and work in a clean environment.
2
Remove Caps and Sterilize Remove plastic caps from both vials. Wipe the rubber stoppers with alcohol swabs and let dry for 30 seconds.
3
Draw BAC Water Using a syringe, draw the calculated amount of BAC water. Use the Reference tab calculator to determine the correct amount.
4
Add Water to Peptide Slowly inject the BAC water down the side of the vial — not directly onto the powder. This prevents protein degradation.
5
Mix Gently Swirl the vial gently in a circular motion. Do not shake. Let sit for 5–10 minutes until fully dissolved.

Storage Guidelines

🧊

Lyophilized (Powder)

  • Freezer (−20°C) or fridge (2–8°C)
  • Lasts 1–2 years when stored properly
  • Keep away from light and moisture
🧪

Reconstituted

  • Must be refrigerated (2–8°C)
  • Use within 30 days (varies by peptide)
  • Never freeze after reconstitution
  • Discard if cloudy, discolored, or has particles

Best Practices

Always use sterile technique
Rotate injection sites
Keep detailed records
Store in original packaging
Check expiration dates
Verify calculations twice
